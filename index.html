How We Met

I met Timo on a spring break camping trip at the Ozarks. It was March, and somehow camping didn't seem like a bad idea...but it was the coldest I have ever been. I only remember Timo as the guy who randomly screamed in the middle of the night. Timo doesn't remember that.

Dating

I wanted to get a meal with my friends before graduation, and Timo was on the list. We cooked lunch and chatted away. My roommates came and went... wondering why we were still eating lunch at 5pm. After weeks of texting, Timo asked me to dinner at Blueberry Hill where it became official!

The Proposal

It was a dark and stormy night. Just kidding, it was a nice and sunny day. I took Shay to the Pavilion, where we had argued about whether or not to go up. Back then, I was tired, it was dark, and there were a lot of mosquitos. But we were finally able to picnic there two years later. While walking the food off, we found a scrapbook of memories. After flipping through it, I looked at her and said, "I know that two years ago I did not want to walk up this hill with you, but I'm willing to climb any mountain or obstacle for you now." I got on one knee and proposed.

Forest Park! The best park in the world in our unbiased opinion. They have paddle boating, Art Museum, Zoo, The Pavilion (Where we got engaged), The Jewel Box, and just a general beautiful scenic view all around.

For food, we would recommend the loop for all kinds of great food. Olivette also has great chinese food such as Cate Zone and Tai Ke!
